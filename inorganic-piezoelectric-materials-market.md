<p><ul><li>Piezoelectric Crystal</li><li>Piezoelectric Ceramics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The inorganic piezoelectric materials market encompasses two main types: piezoelectric crystals and piezoelectric ceramics. Piezoelectric crystals, such as quartz and tourmaline, exhibit piezoelectric properties inherently due to their molecular structure, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. In contrast, piezoelectric ceramics, like lead zirconate titanate (PZT), are man-made materials characterized by their excellent piezoelectric response and can be engineered for specific applications across diverse industries, including automotive, electronics, and healthcare. Both types play crucial roles in energy harvesting, sensors, and actuators.</p></p>
