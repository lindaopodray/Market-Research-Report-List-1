<p><p>The Structured Illumination Microscopy (SIM) market is segmented into two main types: Standard Structured Illumination Microscopy and Superresolution Structured Illumination Microscopy. Standard SIM enhances resolution by using patterned light to illuminate samples, allowing for better imaging of cellular structures. In contrast, Superresolution SIM breaks traditional diffraction limits, achieving higher resolutions by capturing multiple images with varied illumination patterns and reconstructing them. Both methods are crucial in biological and material sciences, enabling detailed visualization without extensive sample preparation.</p></p>
