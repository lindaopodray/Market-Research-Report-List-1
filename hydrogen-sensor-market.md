<p><p>The hydrogen sensor market encompasses several types, each utilizing unique technologies. Electrochemical hydrogen sensors detect hydrogen through a chemical reaction that generates an electrical signal. MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) hydrogen sensors leverage tiny mechanical components to detect gas presence, offering high sensitivity and miniaturization. Chemochromic hydrogen sensors change color in response to hydrogen exposure, providing a visual indication of gas presence. Each type serves distinct applications across industries, ensuring safety and monitoring in environments where hydrogen is prevalent.</p></p>
