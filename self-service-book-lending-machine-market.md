<p><p>The self-service book lending machine market includes two primary types of touch screen technologies: infrared and capacitive. Infrared touch screens utilize beams of light to detect touch, allowing for operation with any object, including gloves. In contrast, capacitive touch screens rely on the electrical properties of the human body to register touch, offering a more responsive and intuitive user experience. Both technologies enhance user interaction, streamline the lending process, and improve access to library resources, catering to diverse user preferences.</p></p>
