<p><p>The firefighting alarm sensors market comprises various sensor types essential for detecting fires. Flame sensors identify the infrared or ultraviolet light emitted by flames, enabling rapid detection. Smoke sensors, which can be ionization or photoelectric, detect smoke particles and provide early alerts. Heat sensors monitor temperature changes, triggering alarms when a specified threshold is met. Light sensors complement these systems by detecting changes in ambient light caused by smoke or flames, enhancing fire detection capabilities and ensuring safety.</p></p>
