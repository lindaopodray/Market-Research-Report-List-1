<p><p>The data storage market comprises three main types: All-Flash Arrays, Hybrid Storage Arrays, and HDD Arrays. All-Flash Arrays utilize only flash memory for high-speed performance, making them ideal for applications requiring low latency and high input/output operations per second. Hybrid Storage Arrays combine flash and traditional hard disk drives (HDDs) to balance performance and cost-effectiveness. HDD Arrays rely solely on traditional spinning disks, offering larger storage capacities at lower prices, suitable for archiving and less performance-intensive applications.</p></p>
