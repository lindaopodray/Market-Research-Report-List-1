<p><p>Linear magnetic encoders are devices used to measure linear position and motion. They come in two main types: Absolute Linear Magnetic Encoders and Incremental Linear Magnetic Encoders. Absolute encoders provide a unique position value immediately upon power-up, ensuring precise location tracking without needing a reference point. Incremental encoders generate pulses relative to movement, requiring a reference point to determine position. Both types are widely used in automation, robotics, and CNC machinery, offering high accuracy, durability, and resistance to environmental factors like dust and vibration.</p></p>
