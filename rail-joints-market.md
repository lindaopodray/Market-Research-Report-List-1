<p><p>The rail joints market includes three main types: standard rail joints, compromise rail joints, and insulated rail joints. Standard rail joints connect two rails to maintain alignment and continuity. Compromise rail joints facilitate the connection of rails of different sizes or weights, ensuring smooth transitions. Insulated rail joints, on the other hand, are designed to electrically isolate sections of track, crucial for signaling systems and reducing interference. Together, these joint types enhance rail efficiency, safety, and maintenance.</p></p>
